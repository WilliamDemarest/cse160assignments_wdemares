Notes to grader:
- Panning with the mouse is based on the distance from the center of the canvas.
There is a dead space in the midle of the canvas where the cursour can rest without movement.
- 'Q' and 'E' control rotation around the forward axis, not panning.
After adding horizontal and vertical panning with the mouse, which also allowed
for 3D movement, this kind of rotaion became very helpful to have and those controlls
are more similar to other aplications I have used.
- Left click to place a block, shift click to remove a block. Overhangs are not supported,
highest block in stack will be increased/decreased. Color/texture is defined by height, so blocks
will not be added if they are above the maximum defined position in the color map for each world.
The block should appear where you are looking, not just the closest block in front of you, but the tracing is not perfect.
- The map can be changed with the "world" buttons. Meadow world is the default and runs smooth
on my device but a little slower with 4x slowdown. Maze world has much less cubes and will run better.
- The "game" is the maze in maze world.
- The maps, especially meadow world, are hollowed out to lower the number of blocks. This process is done
when the map is generated from a png pattern in python, and so will not be updated when blocks are removed.
